##Role of Artificial Intelligence Among SME Food Stalls in Chennai

Project Overview

This project studies the role and impact of Artificial Intelligence (AI) among Small and Medium Enterprise (SME) food stalls in Chennai city. The research focuses on how AI-based tools help food stall owners improve their daily business operations such as billing, inventory management, delivery services, and customer satisfaction.

The study also examines the level of awareness, adoption, and challenges faced by SME food stall owners when using AI technologies.

This project was submitted to the University of Madras as part of the Master of Commerce (M.Com) degree at Women's Christian College, Chennai.

Research Objectives

The main objectives of this research are:

To analyze the role of Artificial Intelligence in improving operational performance of SME food stalls.
To identify the level of awareness and adoption of AI tools among SME food stall owners.
To evaluate the impact of AI on billing, inventory management, and delivery services.
To study the relationship between AI adoption and customer satisfaction.
To examine the challenges and barriers faced while adopting AI technologies.
Methodology

Research Design:
Descriptive and analytical research approach.

Data Collection:

Primary Data: Structured questionnaire
Secondary Data: Journals, research papers, websites

Sample Size:
200 SME food stall owners in Chennai.

Sampling Technique:
Convenience sampling.

Statistical Tools Used:

Cronbach's Alpha (Reliability Test)
Descriptive Analysis
Regression Analysis
One-way ANOVA
AI Tools Studied

The study examines several AI-based tools used by SME food stalls, including:

Petpooja
GoFrugal
Posist
eZee
Vyapar
MyBillBook
Zoho Invoice
Loyverse POS

These tools help businesses manage billing, inventory, customer data, and sales analytics.

Key Findings

The research found that:

AI tools improve operational efficiency and billing accuracy.
AI helps in better inventory management and demand forecasting.
Digital systems increase customer satisfaction and faster service.
Many SME owners still face challenges such as high cost, lack of technical knowledge, and limited awareness.
Limitations of the Study
The research is limited only to SME food stalls in Chennai city.
The study focuses mainly on billing, inventory, delivery, and customer management activities.
Data was collected only once due to time limitations.
